Useful Unix Re-Formatting Commands
wc - word count
  -l: Count lines; -w: Count words; -m: Count characters
tr - translate or delete characters
-d: delete; -t: truncate SET1 to legnth of SET2
SETs are defined by "\<something>"
i.e. \n - newline; \b - backspace; \r - return; \t - tab

sort - Sort
-d: dictionary order; -f: ignore case; -n: numeric sort;
-r: reverse

head & tail - Limit output to beginning or end of file/output
-#: Limit output to '#' lines.
Tail has the "-f" parameter.  Lets you watch a file "grow" as new
entries are added.
